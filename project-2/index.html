Bites, Falls, and Zipline Tragedies

What Vermont’s Workers’ Comp Claims Reveal About On-the-Job Hazards

By Katy Savage

If you get hurt at work — whether it's slipping on a wet floor, getting injured by equipment, or developing a condition like carpal tunnel — workers’ compensation can help cover your medical bills and lost wages while you recover.

In Vermont, thousands of workers have tapped into the system. Over the past five years, there have been 86,437 workers’ compensation claims filed — including 2,089 related to COVID-19.

The data, which spans from January 2020 to the present, offers a revealing look at what kinds of injuries Vermonters are experiencing on the job — and where.

Among the more tragic cases: a 54-year-old man died in 2021 while ziplining at Stowe Mountain Resort.

Animal-related injuries made up a surprising number of claims. There were:

  • 687 dog-related injuries, including 51 sustained by U.S. Postal Service workers
  • 361 tick bite claims, with 164 affecting Vermont state employees
  • 26 cat bites from a single location — Willison Cat

The State of Vermont — which employs around 8,000 people — had the most claims overall, with 3,752.

Next was the University of Vermont Medical Center, with 1,073 claims among its 8,800 employees.

Workplace injuries weren’t just physical. There were 779 claims related to mental health, reflecting the growing toll of emotional strain in the workplace.

Still, the most common injuries were physical:

  • 9,942 falls — the leading type of injury
  • 6,494 cuts and lacerations
